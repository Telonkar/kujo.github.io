A Brief History of Civil Engineering

From ancient pyramids to modern skyscrapers, this is a brief overview of how humans have learned to shape the built world.

Ancient Roman Middle Ages Industrial Modern

Ancient Structures (3000 BCE – 500 BCE)

Early civilizations built some of the most enduring structures in history. The Egyptians constructed the Great Pyramids of Giza around 2560 BCE using limestone blocks weighing up to 80 tons.

Roman Engineering (500 BCE – 500 CE)

The Romans perfected engineering using opus caementicium, enabling structures like the Pantheon and the Colosseum.

Middle Ages & Renaissance (500 – 1700 CE)

Engineers improved structural understanding, and Leonardo da Vinci contributed early conceptual designs.

Industrial Revolution (1750 – 1900)

Major works include the Brooklyn Bridge and the Eiffel Tower. Engineering became formalized through institutions like the Institution of Civil Engineers.

Modern Civil Engineering (1900 – Present)

Projects like the Hoover Dam, Panama Canal, and Channel Tunnel highlight modern capabilities.
