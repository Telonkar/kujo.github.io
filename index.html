Ancient Structures (3000 BCE – 500 BCE)

Early civilizations built some of the most enduring structures in history. The Egyptians constructed the Great Pyramids of Giza around 2560 BCE using limestone blocks weighing up to 80 tons. The Mesopotamians developed irrigation canals and city walls. In Asia, the Indus Valley civilization built well-planned cities with drainage systems. These achievements laid the groundwork for organized construction and engineering.
